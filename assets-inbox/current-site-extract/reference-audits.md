# Reference-brand audits

Three reference homepages audited 2026-05-17 against the Simply Ltd rebuild brief. Each audit captures hero treatment, type system, photography role, catering / franchising surface, one move worth borrowing, one to avoid. Mobile screenshots saved alongside.

---

## 1. Pasta Evangelists — `https://pastaevangelists.com`

Reference brief: catering CTA placement, brand voice, restaurant-grade food photography.

**Hero treatment.** No video. Hero is a large editorial-grade photographic crop — top-down plates of fresh pasta on warm, slightly grainy backgrounds, with restrained typographic overlays. The homepage opens with a four-card "how to enjoy" grid (`Dine in one of our fresh pasta restaurants` / `Enjoy our fresh pasta for delivery` / `Learn to make pasta with our expert chefs` / `Order Pasta Evangelists for catering`) — every card carries its own photo. Catering is one of the four primary first-fold paths, not a buried link.

**Typography system.** Display: `Fortescue Median` (humanist serif, used for hero headlines, scroll-stops, and section heads). Body: `Basic Sans` (geometric humanist sans). The display is set in mixed-case at modest weights (300–400) — restraint and confidence, not shouting. Buttons use the serif too, which is unusual but works.

**Photography role.** Photography is the protagonist. Every block on the homepage is anchored by a photographic asset — top-down plate shots, kitchen-action shots, a chef teaching at the Pasta Academy. Almost no decorative illustration. Even the press logos (Dragons' Den, Harrods) are SVG marks set quietly.

**Catering / franchising surface.** Catering is exposed directly in the primary nav AND repeated as one of four first-fold "ways to enjoy" cards. Clicking through to `/pages/catering` reveals four sub-tiers (Private, Wedding, Corporate, Party) each with its own photo and pitch. The catering form / enquiry CTA sits at the bottom of each tier page — the page sells the proposition before asking for the enquiry. Exactly the "earn the enquiry before asking for it" pattern the Simply Ltd brief calls for.

**Borrow.** The four-card "ways to enjoy" first-fold layout — each card a photographic tile, each one a distinct conversion path (dine in / collect / delivery / catering). Maps cleanly to Simply Ltd's four primary audiences (catering enquirers, franchisees, walk-up customers, repeat customers) without forcing a single CTA.

**Don't borrow.** The "Join Pasta Evangelists Family" newsletter modal that fires on first paint — interrupts the hero before the visitor has seen anything, hurts the first impression and undermines the photography it covers up.

## 2. Honest Burgers — `https://honestburgers.co.uk`

Reference brief: menu presentation, restaurant photography, location finder UX.

**Hero treatment.** Static photographic hero. A press image of the brand winning the National Burger Award is the lead asset, layered over a dark warm wood/restaurant backdrop. The headline `GREAT BRITISH BURGERS` is set in a heavy condensed display sans, all-caps, white on dark. Confident, declarative, single message. Below the hero, a hero-sized photographic crop of a burger (filename `triple_014-1.jpg`) anchors the next section.

**Typography system.** Display: `Ko` (with `Impact` fallback) — a heavy condensed grotesque, set ALL-CAPS at weights 400 and 900, used for every heading, button, and most nav items. Body: `Gi` (sans-serif, body weight 400). The hierarchy is one display face doing nearly all the work, body face only for paragraphs. Very British-restaurant signage feel.

**Photography role.** Photography is the protagonist and is **restaurant-grade**: tight crops of the burger, hand-held shots showing the bite/sear/melt, occasional wide environment shots. Customer faces appear sparingly. Food close-ups dominate. No illustration anywhere.

**Catering / franchising surface.** Primary nav: `Menu / Locations / Book / About / Order Online / Shop`. Catering is NOT in the primary nav (it lives deep in About). The site is built for walk-up + repeat customers, not enquirers. Useful negative reference: this is what *not* to do if catering is the conversion target — Honest Burgers can afford to bury it because they own physical sites; Simply Ltd cannot.

**Borrow.** The single-condensed-display-face approach paired with restaurant-grade food photography in the hero. Tells a confident, food-first story without any decoration. Specifically: the tight burger-crop hero shot beneath the headline — Simply Burgers should have a direct equivalent.

**Don't borrow.** Setting **every** heading, **every** button, and **most** nav items in heavy all-caps condensed display. At Honest Burgers' scale (a single concept) it reads as identity; for Simply Ltd's multi-brand structure it would steamroll the sub-brand voices the rebuild brief explicitly wants to differentiate.

## 3. MEATliquor — `https://meatliquor.com`

Reference brief: visual identity, type, restraint vs. attitude.

**Hero treatment.** No video. Hero is a hand-drawn / chalk-block layered composition: a bright yellow display headline `BURGERS, BOOZE & BAD DECISIONS` set on a red rough-edged ribbon, paired with a slightly grainy lifestyle photograph (a hand holding a pint of stout, burgers in soft focus). The page background carries a film-grain noise texture (`noise.png` as a tiling background image), giving the whole homepage a slightly distressed, fanzine-ish atmosphere. The subhead works as origin-story headline copy: `FROM A VAN IN A PECKHAM CAR PARK TO A JOINT NEAR YOU - WE'VE BEEN SMASHING BURGERS, SHAKING COCKTAILS & REWRITING THE RULES SINCE DAY ONE.`

**Typography system.** Display: `shelton` (a chunky display sans/grotesque), used for nearly all headings — weights 600 and 900, mix of upper-case and title-case. Body: `Roboto Slab` for some paragraph blocks; the rest falls back to system stacks (Apple/Segoe/Roboto). Buttons set in `shelton` 600 uppercase. Identity does **not** rely on premium custom serifs — it commits to one personality face and one slab body and lets attitude do the lifting.

**Photography role.** Photography is supporting, not protagonist. Composition is led by the typography and the chalk-block layout devices. Photos appear in modest crops, often at half-screen widths, and many homepage tiles use solid coloured backgrounds with photographic insets rather than full-bleed photographic heroes. This is the "restraint via attitude not minimalism" mode — busy with personality, but not noisy with photography.

**Catering / franchising surface.** Primary nav: `Find Us / Menu / Deals / Book Now / Sign Up / Gift Vouchers / Careers`. Order Online is the loudest CTA (red, top-right). No catering CTA visible from the homepage. Like Honest Burgers, MEATliquor is a walk-up + repeat business, not an enquiry-driven business — useful only as a tonal reference, not a structural one.

**Borrow.** The origin-story-as-subhead move — one line that condenses the "how it started" into the homepage frame without needing a separate "Our Story" section. Owner has a real founding-in-2019 narrative ("a single food truck: Simply Falafel"); a tight, declarative version in the hero subhead would do a lot of work.

**Don't borrow.** Tiling film-grain noise across the entire body background. At Lighthouse-≥95 mobile and with prefers-reduced-motion respected, the perceptual cost of a noisy texture across every section is not worth the atmosphere. Use noise as a sparing accent if at all.
